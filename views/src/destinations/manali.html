MANALI

where heaven comes to you

MANALI
The hill station of Manali is a charming town situated in the northern end of Kullu valley with a growing number of tourists contributing to Manali tourism. Manali, Himachal Pradesh once served as the ancient trade route between India and China. Today it is an abode for modern creativity with astounding hills and adventurous activities. While planning a Manali trip, know that this town is a true haven for culture lovers, foodies, adventure junkies, and peace seekers. The high Manali altitude allows you to experience the perfect Himalayan ambience and lets you cruise your way through the snowy roads of the famous Rohtang Pass. There is also a lot to do for people who wish to travel back in time and indulge in the exquisite display of culture and heritage at the Museum of Himachal Culture & Folk Art during their Manali trip. The mighty Himalayas have inspired many foreign settlements here, giving rise to popular European and Israeli cafes, restaurants and hostels, providing one a consortium of around the world cultures. This town is a true haven for adventure junkies who can indulge in river rafting, paragliding, camping, rock climbing, rappelling, zorbing at Solang Valley and Aleo. Manali tourism also has an array of breathtaking treks and sights for its nature lovers, for instance, the Patalsu Peak, the Deo Tibba base camp, Jogini Falls and the Rahala Falls. For all the solo riders out there, cruise your way through the snowy alley of Rohtang Pass while those who wish to travel back in time can indulge in the exquisite display of culture and heritage at the Museum of Himachal Culture and Folk Art. Restaurants and cafes such as the Khyber Pass, Johnson's Cafe, La Plage, Drifters' Inn, The Hangout attract foodies for their culinary justice to everything from Thai to European cuisines and even some live music. If all this is too over the top for you, then reconnect with simplicity at Naggar Village, which is home to waterfalls, a beautiful castle, an art gallery and locals which have many stories to share and a cultural heritage to take pride in. Manali tourism has always been on the higher side because of Manali’s favourable weather conditions and picturesque scenery.

This 400-year-old temple, protected by a dense cedar forest, is one of the unmissable places to visit in Manali. The history, beauty and peace surrounding this temple is what makes it such a wonderful tourist location. The temple is dedicated to the demon princess Hidimba Devi. As per the Mahabharata, Bhima married Hidimba after defeating her brother in a fight. Hidimba stayed back in Manali after the Pandavas left to rule the people of her kingdom. Later in life, she renounced the throne and took to meditating on a rock in the forest. This rock is still a part of the temple and is revered as the spot where Hidimba became a goddess. There is a temple devoted to Ghatotkach, her son, near the Hidimba Devi temple. The architecture of the temple, with pagodas, is a matter of intrigue to visitors. The place is dark and quiet, and is one of the best places to visit in Manali for a peaceful experience.

Jogini Falls is located at a height of 160 feet, a 45-minute trek from the Vashisht Temple. The journey to the waterfall, which takes you through thick forests of pine and apple orchards, is exceptionally beautiful. You will know why this waterfall is called one of the most magnificent places to visit in Manali when you reach the base of the Jogini Falls. Crystal clear waters gushing down the craggy mountains, this waterfall offers a spectacular view. The small pool of water accumulated below the falls is a perfect spot for tourists to go for a quick dip. There is a temple dedicated to Yogini Mata up top. If you wish to experience the best of the place, do visit during the months of March to May, and October to February. If you feel adventurous enough, go for a trek to the upper portion of the Jogini Falls. Although difficult, the view from the top is straight out of a fairytale. One of the most popular places to visit in Manali, Jogini Falls should definitely be on your list.

If you are in Himachal Pradesh, then this exciting place to visit in Manali should definitely be on your list. Known to be the adventure lovers’ paradise, Solang Valley offers a wide array of adventure sports for you to choose from. A famous Manali tourist place, visitors flock here from every part of the country to enjoy their days and return with colourful memories. Falling on the way to Rohtang from Manali, Solang Valley is a place best known for paragliding. You will be trained on how to properly use the harness by professionals. Once you are ready, be prepared to glide through the mighty hills and witness the thick pine forests and green slopes from up above. The best time to paraglide is from June to May and October to December. It doesn’t stop at that – Solang Valley provides you with horse riding, parachuting and zorbing facilities. Have you ever thought that tumbling down the slopes of the Himalayas would be enjoyable? Well, now you will, because zorbing makes that fun.

The Mall Road is one of the most lively places to visit in Manali. Crowded and noisy, the Mall Road stands as a contrast to the otherwise peaceful town of Manali. The streets are lined with shops and stalls on either sides, so keep your eyes peeled for things that attract you. For those of you who like to haggle, your skills will be rewarded here. Famous for Kashmiri shawls, winter clothes, sarees, and various antique items, visiting this Manali tourist place should definitely top your list.

Situated in a cosy location beside pine trees, Casa Belle Vista is a vegetarian restaurant run by a Spanish-Indian couple. The French onion soup here is a must try, along with their delectable wood-fired pizza. Apart from the food and natural decor what makes Casa Belle Vista such a wonderful place to visit in Manali is the friendly staff. When you are here, do not forget to sip on some wine or fruity mocktail and unwind with the sound of the gushing Manalsu River.
