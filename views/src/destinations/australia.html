AUSTRALIA
Australia, the world's smallest continent and largest island, is a paradise for travellers. Beautiful beaches, rugged mountains, verdant rainforests and even cosmopolitan cities are the major attractions of Australia tourism. The country is made up of eight states and territories – Australian Capital Territory (ACT), New South Wales (NSW), Northern Territory (NT), Queensland (QLD), South Australia (SA), Tasmania (TAS), Victoria (VIC) and Western Australia (WA). The coastline covers almost 60,000km. You can spend your time in the thriving cities of Australia and can also set out on an epic journey along the awe inspiring coastline of Australia tourism. The adventure spirit in you will enjoy a drive on the Great Ocean Road or a hike in the Blue Mountains National Park. From diving into the Great Barrier Reef to watching the sunset over Uluru, there are no dearth of activities in this enormous and beautiful country. The cultural and culinary delights of Australia tourism are unforgettable too. Australia is one of the best destinations for foodies – from kangaroo steaks to freshly shucked oysters, a food adventure is waiting for you at every corner. Melbourne is also home to great coffee. Australia has always been a producer of good quality gin, rum and whisky for decades. The single malt whiskey of Tasmania and cinnamon gin of Adelaide are world famous.

Just in case you feel the need to take a break from the cultural capital of Australia and experience what it is like beyond amazing coffee and the tram life, these are the places you need to be at.

Not just within Australia, but, one of the world’s most famous drive. 2 hours drive time from Melbourne city this scenic road is full of breath taking surprises at almost every turn. From peaceful white sand beaches to charming little towns and dense rain forests, it has it all. If you are lucky you’d be able to spot some wild kangaroos while crossing the forest.

Escape to Yarra Valley to indulge in wine and gastronomic experiences. This pristine and rustic wine region has a cool climate region and is only 1-hour drive from Melbourne city. Visit the charming village of Marysville up north to see the lake mountain and Stevenson waterfalls. Sample the grapes and enjoy a selection of produce from wine to food within the wineries all while enjoying a stunning view of the vineyards.

A white resort village located on the Alpine region of Australia which is a 3 hours drive from Melbourne city in Victoria. The village is known for its ski slopes, terrain parks and pristine white snow around season. An alpine retreat where you can wake up to some stunning snowy landscapes.

The mountains are not naturally blue here, it’s the just that the mountains at a distance gives off an illusion of them being different shades of blue, in real they are grey like any other mountain. Waterfalls, steep cliffs, canyons, eucalyptus forest and charming villages with little cafes and guesthouse makes taking a trip to the Blue Mountains and a bushwalk (hike) a lot worth while.
