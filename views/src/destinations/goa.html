GOA

A kaleidoscopic blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures

GOA
Famous for its beaches, Goa is the smallest state in India in terms of area, the fourth smallest state in terms of the population yet a huge contributor to the economy through Goa tourism. Panaji is the capital of Goa. Goa Tourism is considered to be the major support to Goa’s economy. Through Tripoto’s Goa tourism and travel guide you can explore the best of Goa. Thinking of ‘where to go in Goa’? Check out the below Goa tour guide and get all the necessary information about Goa tourism. When in India’s sunshine state, be prepared to fall in love with sandy beaches, palm-fringed shores and beachfront Portuguese bungalows. Goa will never fail to entice you with its landscapes peppered with paddy fields, coconut groves, windy cliffs and riverside forts. While the southern part of the state is a haven for tourists seeking quiet days spent by the tranquil seaside, Goa tourism in the north is known primarily because of its bustling shacks and legendary nightlife. From the umpteen number of places to visit in Goa, a few that most definitely shouldn’t be missed are the Fontainhas – India’s only existing Latin quarters, the 17th century old Bom Jesus Basilica; to revel in a little bohemian spirit, the vibrant Wednesday flea market at Anjuna Beach, and Dudhsagar – one of India’s highest waterfalls. But if you’re here simply to unwind, there’s no dearth of things to enjoy.

Located in North Goa, Baga is one of the most popular places to visit in Goa. Visited by travellers from across the world, the beach offers a plethora of activities which makes it one of the top beaches in Goa. Baga is home to popular cafes and restaurants and one can easily spend a day here enjoying the warm sunshine, great food and numerous water sports. We suggest heading here late in the afternoon to enjoy a lavish lunch at Britto’s or Go With the Flow. Offering local Goan cuisine, Mughlai, Indian and Chinese, the restaurants here are an ideal way to enjoy the popular Goan vibe. If you head here in the evening, you can enjoy live music as well as admire the beach under the soft shimmer of the moonlight.

Butterfly Beach in South Goa is counted among the most beautiful places to visit in Goa. The beach gets its unique name from the surrounding trees which act as a magnet for a huge variety of butterflies and also owing to its unique butterfly shape. Fondly known as Honeymoon Beach, it is one of the lesser-visited beaches in Goa owing to lack of accessibility. The best way to reach the beach is via a ferry which is operated from Palolem Beach. If you are travelling by car, you can reach the periphery of the beach but will have to trek to reach the main shoreline. In fact, the beach is one of the most popular places to visit in Goa for trekkers and hikers owing to lush forests surrounding the picturesque beach.

Palolem is one of the most popular places to visit in Goa, especially if picturesque views and surreal beauty is what makes a holiday perfect for you. Situated in South Goa, it has gradually made it to the list of the best places to visit in Goa and attracts visitors from all over the world. Home to a plethora of cafes, homestays and beach shacks, Palolem is an ideal place to spend your time in Goa. Owing to the array of beautiful resorts here, the beach is a haven for leisure travellers, couples and families. On the other hand, if you are travelling on a budget, you’ll find several homestays, shacks and hotels lining the beach. Ideal for sunbathing, yoga and cafe hopping, Palolem is where you can board a ferry for dolphin-viewing cruises as well as to the popular Butterfly Beach.

Considered to be one of the largest churches in Asia, Se Cathedral is one of the top places to visit in Goa. Visited by travellers from all over the world, the church is known for its beauty and architectural simplicity. The cathedral has primarily gained importance as one of the centres of the Latin Rite Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Goa and Daman. The church took almost 80 years to be constructed and was primarily established to celebrate the victory of the Portuguese over Muslims rulers. A perfect example of Portuguese-Manueline architecture, the church is one of its kind in India and is a must include in your Goa itinerary. Another striking feature about the cathedral is that it has the biggest bell in Goa and one of the biggest in Asia. According to locals, the bell can be heard across the state and the sound is almost musical.

Ask anyone about their favourite places to visit in Goa and chances are that Thalassa will be one of the top five. A dreamy ambience, breathtaking views of the sea and delicious food have made this a favourite with travellers and locals alike. White dominates the colour theme and goes perfectly with the soothing music and excited chatter of guests. With a predominantly Greek menu, Thalassa is a must-try if you are looking for a break from local Goan cuisine and everyday Italian food.
